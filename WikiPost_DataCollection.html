Wiki Post - Data Collection Tools

Data collection tools suitable for the research investigation on the research proposal:“Implementing Cyber Security tools and/or techniques for the reduction of Cyberbullying - Tools and/or techniques for the reducing Cyber-bullying”

The concept of cyberbullying is an intense issue, and mounting with time, it occurs in different ways than it did when it initially surfaced. With advances in technology, especially with the flexibility of Smart Phones, Tools and/or techniques for reducing Cyberbullying should be effective with the upgrading technology.

How will you collect it and what analysis would you hope to perform?

The analysis will be conducted with a mixed methods explanatory design (Creswell & Clark, 2007). In two phases, phase one consists of quantitative data collection; phase two involves collecting qualitative data that links the data from phase one (Creswell & Clark, 2007). The participant selection model of the explanatory design will be used. The model involves quantitative data obtained from the first phase to decisively select the participants for interviews for the next phase of the qualitative portion (Creswell & Clark, 2007).

The quantitative data for the study is done through a crosssectional survey, collecting all the data at a single point to understand the current views on the issue (Creswell,2008). The survey aims to determine Tools and/or techniques for reducing Cyberbullying.

The qualitative data obtained in this study are from interviews conducted with the participants who were identified during the first phase of the study. The participants in phase two are selected through purposeful sampling, as their survey data will indicate an existing effective policy related to cyberbullying (Creswell, 2008). A phenomenological approach is employed to understand the aspect of the phenomenon faced by each individual. Each participant selected will be engaged in a one-on-one interview with the researcher. The interview involves open-ended questions related to their approach to cyberbullying, as Open-ended questions help the participant to explain their stories without restriction (Creswell, 2008).

The results of the qualitative portion of the study will identify the major essential for effective cyberbullying Tools and/or techniques, specifically:

  • The importance of cyberbullying reporting procedure;
  • Cyberbullying prevention training integration;
  • Victim-centered approach
  • Medium of cyberbullying prevention training such as Broadcast Journalism;
  • A focus on prevention rather than punishment;
  • The importance of punishment as a part of formulating effective policy;
  • Coping with the changes in technology.

The issue will be understood from the perspective of addressing the consequence effectively with the actions directly related to cyberbullying.

What all are the different types of tools or resources available to resist Cyberbullying?

-Safety website

Stopcyberbullying website with the extension of .com, and .org, is a website of wiredsaferty with the information on identifying cyberbullying and preventing it. Further, it provides information on how to handle cyberbullying.

Stopcyberbullying website with the extension of .govt is a government website that is loaded with a lot of information on both bullying and cyberbullying.

"A Thin Line" - Athinline is the website with the extension of .org that provides facts on digital abuse and cyberbullying, helping the visitors to learn how to deal with the situation.

CyberSmart website with an extension .org is an MTV initiative along with "A Thin Line", to end digital abuse; the network enlists cybercrime experts, psychologists, and youth activists (Whelan D L, 2011).

Many websites like MySpace and other social media portals allow users to report abusers and bullies by accessing a link in the user profile. Facebook has an option to e-mail the instances at abuse@facebook.com to report any misuse of their portal.

-Netiquette

Netiquette is a set of rules for Internet etiquette, listed on the metsmartz website with an extension .org, focusing on reducing disinhibited behavior online (Shea S E, 1994). The iSafe Internet Safety Program (i-SAFE Inc, 1998, 2009) is a subscription-based prevention program for students in Grades K–12. Further, the program is available via DVD Nersmarta or in-person training, the parents and community leaders are provided with a professional development program for combating cyberbullying.

-Applications- Bully Block app.

The Bully Block app helps the user to block unwanted text messages, calls, and pictures for Android phones. With the help of the app, one can also record a secret file and send the information to the victim's trusted person (Poland S, 2010).

Despite the above measures, it is widely recommended that the victims start talking about the incidences of bullying to a person with whom they are comfortable and report such instances immediately (Frey K S, Hirschstein M K, Edstrom L V, & Snell J L, 2009)
