Literature Review Outline:Inappropriate use of surveys

Case Study: Cambridge Analytica misusing on Facebook user’s data:

Cambridge Analytica is a federal marketing, data analytics, and consulting business located in London, United Kingdom. The company has facilities in London, New York, and Washington, DC. Cambridge Analytica has been accused of obtaining Facebook data inappropriately and planning a variety of national crusades. [1]

These crusades include American Senator Ted Cruz and, to a lesser extent, Donald Trump and the Leave-EU Brexit campaign that resulted in the UK's withdrawal from the EU.

In spring 2018, the Facebook–Cambridge Analytica data scandal was a massive setback. Cambridge Analytica obtained the private data of millions of people's Facebook profiles without their permission and used it for Political Advertising. It was dubbed a "watershed moment" in the country's knowledge of private data, prompting a 17% drop in Facebook's cut rate and calls for stricter laws governing tech companies' use of personal data. [1]

Methodology:

Data Collection:

CA collaborated with Aleksandr Kogan, a UK research professor, to develop a Facebook quiz app to understand user psychology. Facebook's terms of service at the time indicated that the developer was entitled to harvest user data for research purposes but did not specify whether the developer was also permitted to gather the data of the user's friends. [2]

Users were required to log in to their Facebook accounts to complete the survey. Users would have to authorize the App to access their user data when they log into the App. Although they only approved data collection for their user data, they unintentionally allowed data collection for their friend's user data as well. [2]

Their information includes their name, gender, location, ethnicity, education level, favorite pages, clothing brand, etc. The professor/CA was able to gather information from 75 million nodes. [2]

Data Management:

User profiles were established for each person based on the data obtained, and they have been categorized accordingly. The user profiles were created based on where they lived in the United States. Users' political affinities were deduced based on the brand of clothes they wore. As a result, user profiles were further refined based on their beliefs, such as conservative or liberal.

Based on OCEAN scores, classification algorithms were employed to categorize user profiles. The OCEAN scores represent the user's level of Openness, Conscientiousness, Extroversion, Agreeableness, and Neuroticism. [2]

Machine Learning:

Regression was used to build a Machine Learning model on accessible data sets to predict the political affiliation of a new user. The user's profile, including psychographic data, their Facebook activity, and the OCEAN score, was combined to forecast political allegiance information. The user's concerns about specific policies and the improvements the user would want to see the government adopt were taken into account by the Machine Learning Model. [2]

Micro-targeting:

The Model's output is utilized to construct a good Campaign. Advertisements are displayed to people based on their political preferences. Advertisements with psychographic micro-targeting are presented so that they either strengthen or reinforce a user's preconceived notions about a particular party. The advertising is well-tailored to the psychographics of the users, intending to achieve maximum results. [2]

Example:

Users who lived in border areas were concerned about immigration. Therefore they were grouped with anti-immigration voters. Users in the hinterlands were worried about job losses and the development of oil and gas pipelines through Native American settlements. Therefore they were grouped while creating profiles. Net Worth: Extremely High Tax breaks were a significant issue for those living in wealthy suburbs or downtown regions. Thus it was a substantial component in their profile construction. [1]

If a user is worried about immigration policy (Anti-immigration User Profile Class), advertising detailing how a specific party is working in that area will be targeted. [1]

Breach Consequences:

In March 2018, Christopher Wylie, a former GSR employee, revealed the data collecting operation. Many users were concerned and demanded stricter restrictions on their personal information. Others were even considering deactivating their Facebook accounts.

Indeed, with the viral hashtag #deletefacebook, a rising campaign to delete Facebook quickly spread worldwide. Financially, Facebook's stock price dropped 7% the day following the incident, and it's market worth fell by more than $36 billion. [4]

Facebook’s Reaction:

Facebook's initial reaction on the day Wylie's revelations were made public was to deny how the press presented the event. On the network's blog, Paul Grewal, the company's deputy general counsel, stated that Aleksandr Kogan solicited and obtained information from users who decided to sign up for his App and that everyone engaged granted their agreement defended the social media's soft rules. [3]

Later, the business realized that blaming customers for not comprehending its complicated privacy rules was not the most excellent strategy, especially given the public outcry. On March 21, five days after the CA data scandal was revealed, Mark Zuckerberg broke his silence. [3]

Users were warned that the access they had allowed to various programs would be withdrawn and examined in the settings, in addition to their audit trials on breach investigation. Meanwhile, Facebook has pledged to create an app that would require users to erase all of their Facebook web search data. [4]

Changes in Policy:

Logical data security regulations became effective across Europe on May 25, 2018, through The General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) became effective. It implies to all companies that prepare private data about EU citizens, regardless of their location. Processing is a general phrase that refers to everything linked to personal data, such as how a company handles and uses data, including settling, preserving, using, and destroying it. [4]

While many of the GDPR's provisions are based on EU data protection legislation, the GDPR has a more extensive reach, more specific restrictions, and substantial penalties. [4]

Though specific programs may undergo modifications or upgrades, the platform user should be aware of the types of personal data and applications to which they should provide rights.
