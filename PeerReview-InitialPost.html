Peer Review : Initial Post Dark UX Patterns - Anrich Potgieter

This post describes the ACM Code of Ethics and the BCS Code of Conduct in establishing a solid basis of ethical behavior and decision-making in computing using an ACM case study on the Dark UX pattern.

The author demonstrates how Dark UX patterns misled design patterns to lead people to browse or execute actions in a graphical user interface that they didn't want to do.

According to the case study, senior executives demanded Dark UX modifications to their e-commerce website to increase refunds due to user mistakes. Furthermore, despite his concerns, the colleague entrusted with making these system adjustments followed his line manager's instructions and made the necessary changes to the GUI.

The author meticulously recorded the changes made by senior executives and linked each change to a breach of the AMC and BCS codes of conduct. To help the reader comprehend the code, the author has provided the concept and sections of the AMC and BCS codes.

Finally, the author concludes the paper by outlining the legal operation of whistleblowing.

Conclusion:

The author well-researched the sources and delivered a well-written essay. The author created material that was devoid of grammatical errors and easy to read. The author was successful in conveying the article's context.
